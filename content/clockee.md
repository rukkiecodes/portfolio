## What Clockee Is

It replaces paper sign-in sheets, easily-shared PIN pads, and "honour system" spreadsheets with a verified digital record of every clock-in and clock-out — backed by location, a live photo, and a unique on-site code so the record can be trusted. Staff use a simple phone app to start and end their workday in seconds; managers get a real-time, tamper-resistant view of who's in, who's late, who's on overtime, and who never showed up. And because Clockee was built for the real world — where staff are often on their feet in places with weak or no internet — it keeps working even when the network doesn't.

Clockee serves businesses and institutions that depend on people physically showing up: schools, clinics and hospitals, factories and warehouses, security firms, hospitality, retail chains, NGOs, and field-service teams. For each of them it turns attendance from a source of disputes and guesswork into a clean, auditable system of record.

## The Problem Clockee Solves

Most organisations still track attendance in ways that are slow, easy to cheat, or both. The pain shows up in five recurring ways:

- **"Buddy punching" and fake attendance.** With sign-in books or shared PINs, one person can clock in for a colleague who isn't there. Payroll then pays for hours nobody worked, and there's no way to prove it after the fact.
- **No proof of presence.** Even when someone does clock in, there's rarely evidence they were at the right *place*. A staff member can mark themselves present from home, from the road, or from anywhere.
- **Disputes over hours.** "I came in at 8, not 8:40." Without an objective record, every late arrival, early exit, or missed shift becomes an argument — and a manager's word against an employee's.
- **Manual, error-prone timesheets.** Compiling attendance by hand at the end of the month is tedious, mistake-ridden, and impossible to audit. Overtime and penalties get miscalculated, and payroll inherits the mess.
- **Connectivity that can't be relied on.** Many workplaces — rural schools, clinics, construction sites, factory floors — have patchy or no internet exactly where and when staff need to clock in. Tools that assume a constant connection simply fail there.

Clockee addresses all five directly. Every attendance record carries proof — where the person was, a live photo of who they are, and confirmation they were physically on site — and the whole thing works whether or not there's a signal. The result is attendance you can actually trust, and far fewer arguments about it.

## How It Works, From the User's Point of View

For staff, Clockee is intentionally simple. They open the app, see their current shift, and tap to clock in. Depending on how their organisation has set things up, the app confirms their attendance using one or more quick checks — taking a live selfie, confirming they're within the workplace's location, or scanning a code posted on site. A few seconds later they're clocked in, with a verified record created automatically. At the end of the day they clock out the same way, and their hours are calculated for them.

For managers, the experience is about visibility and control. From an administrative view they can see attendance across all staff in real time, review who was on time versus late, spot missed clock-outs, configure the rules that matter to them, and pull reports for payroll or compliance — without chasing anyone or compiling anything by hand.

## Verified, Fraud-Resistant Attendance

The heart of Clockee — and what sets it apart from a basic time clock — is that every attendance record is **verified**, not just recorded. Organisations can combine several layers of proof, switching each on or off to match their needs:

- **Location confirmation.** Clockee checks that the staff member is physically within the workplace's boundary when they clock in or out. Someone can't mark themselves present from home or from across town. If a clock-in happens outside the expected area, it's flagged for a manager to review rather than quietly accepted.
- **Live photo / liveness.** A real, in-the-moment photo (not a stored image) confirms *who* is clocking in, eliminating buddy punching. Nobody can clock in for an absent colleague.
- **On-site code.** Staff can be required to scan a unique code posted at the workplace, tying the clock-in to that specific physical location.
- **Supervised proxy clock-in.** For legitimate cases where a staff member genuinely can't use their own device, an authorised supervisor can clock them in on their behalf — with a recorded reason and full accountability, so the exception is documented rather than hidden.

Because these checks happen at the moment of clocking in, and because the final decision on whether an attendance is valid is made centrally, the record an organisation ends up with is one it can stand behind — in a payroll dispute, a disciplinary case, or a compliance audit.

## Built to Work Anywhere — Even Offline

A defining promise of Clockee is that **it keeps working when the internet doesn't.** This is not a footnote; for many of Clockee's users it's the whole reason they can adopt it at all.

When a staff member is somewhere with no signal — a basement clinic, a rural school, a warehouse dead zone — they can still clock in and out normally. The app captures everything it needs, including the verification photo and location, and safely holds onto it on the device. The staff member can even see their pending clock-ins so they know it worked. As soon as the phone reconnects, Clockee quietly syncs everything to the central system, preserving the *original* time it happened rather than the time it synced. From the worker's perspective, it just works; from the organisation's perspective, no attendance is ever lost and no timestamp is ever fudged.

This offline-first reliability means Clockee fits the messy reality of frontline work, instead of demanding perfect conditions that those workplaces can't provide.

## Beyond Clock-In: Shifts, Overtime & Fair Penalties

Clockee covers the full shape of a working day, not just the moment of arrival:

- **Shifts and schedules.** Staff see their current, upcoming, and active shifts, so expectations are clear. Reminders help them remember to clock in and out at the right times.
- **Overtime.** Extra hours are captured and reviewed properly, so people are credited for the time they actually put in.
- **Penalties and pardons.** Organisations can define their own rules for lateness, early departure, or missed clock-outs, and Clockee applies them consistently and transparently — the same rules for everyone. When there's a genuine reason, a manager can pardon a penalty, keeping the system both fair and humane.

Instead of attendance being a blunt "present or absent" flag, Clockee gives a nuanced, honest picture of each person's reliability over time — on-time rates, lateness, overtime, and absences — that managers can act on.

Staff can check a pickup against the approved list, and the school keeps a clear history of every pickup — completed, pending, cancelled, or expired. The same trust that protects payroll is turned toward something far more important: child safety at the school gate.

Alongside this, Clockee's broader education tools support student attendance and assessment workflows, letting schools manage more of their daily operations in one place.
