## What Quantlow is

Quantlow is an AI chart-analysis tool. Point it at any trading chart — forex, crypto, stocks, or indices — and it returns a clear, structured verdict in one click: **BUY** or **SELL**, an entry, a stop, a target, the key levels around price, a confidence score and grade, and a plain-English explanation of the reasoning behind the call.

It comes in two parts that work together: a web app, where you upload or scan a chart, review the verdict, argue with it, and save it to your journal; and a browser companion that reads the chart on the tab you're already on — like a broker's terminal — and shows the same structured verdict in a side panel, including a countdown to the exact moment to enter.

## What each scan gives you

Every scan produces a complete, reviewable trade idea rather than a vague signal:

- **Direction & conviction** — a BUY or SELL verdict with a confidence percentage and letter grade, so you know how strongly the setup reads.
- **The full trade** — entry, stop, target, and the key levels around current price: the numbers you actually need to place and manage the trade.
- **Reasoning you can read** — a short, human explanation of *why*: trend, structure, momentum, and demand and supply zones. A second opinion you can judge, not a black box.
- **Invalidation** — a clear condition that voids the setup, so you know exactly when the idea is wrong.
- **Timing** — in live mode it counts down to the next candle, telling you when to enter, with an optional alert.

## More than one-off scans

Quantlow keeps a record. A dashboard summarises recent activity and trade feedback, every scan is saved to a history you can revisit, and you can mark how each trade actually closed — win, loss, or draw — turning the tool into a trading journal that grows alongside you. Verdicts can also be delivered straight to Telegram.

## Who it's for

Active traders who want a fast, structured second opinion without leaving their chart — a disciplined, repeatable read on any setup, with direction, entry, risk, and reasoning laid out in seconds instead of minutes.
